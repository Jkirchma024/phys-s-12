Week 1: Final Project Proposal

Idea 1: Remote Controlled Ping-Pong Scoreboard

When playing ping-pong it is very easy to lose track of score and it is annoying to use any scoring app since it requires going to your phone after every rally and when one person controls the scoreboard cheating is easily possible. (especially with my friends)

My solution would be a physical scoreboard that mounts to the table and is controlled by remote controls that both players would have. This remote control would change the score (inc/dec) and in order to prevent cheating both players must make an input unanimously on their respective controllers for the score change to occur.

The scoreboard will be elevated with the elecronic components hidden in in a compartment behind the scoreboard face.

The mount used would be the same/similar to the clamp down mounts ping-pong nets typically use. There will be 2 mounts, each on one side of the net.

This would be roughly how the remote controller looks (ideally pocket sized).

Idea 2: Liquid Usage Sensor

Before I go to the store I like to check my fridge to see what needs a replenishment. However, it is impossible to see how much is left in certian containers that are not transparent, and I am often too lazy to reach all the way back to check for myself.

My solution to this would be a weight sensor that mounts at the bottom of a liquid container: milk carton, water jugs, etc. The user would put this sensor on a new container and would input the volume of liquid inside (on the label of the container). Then after each use of the substance inside, the sensor would work with a microcontroller to calculate the amount just used and the amount remaining. The microcontroller could also convert the displayed data to various units of measure. This data would be displayed on a screen and could potentially connect to a mobile application that would inform users what they need more of remotely.

This is an example of how the device would look when mounted to a container. If possible I would love the base of the device to be adjustable to fit various container sizes.

Idea 3: Garden Pot Irrigation Ring

My third idea is an irrigation system for garden pots that clips onto the edge of the pot. There would be pipes that aim water into the soil of the pot. The activation of water dispensing from the ends of these pipes could be triggered remotely or be based on a timer system. The dispening mechanism would be some sort of valve or hole that is able to be closed/opened, all proccessed through a microcontroller. However, the main challenge I see with this project is managing the wiring in a way that avoids touching the water.
